Catchment Scale Geomorphic Relationships


  The upper Narraguagus River does not fit the classic model of "erosional-transport-depositional" zones from upstream to downstream. Rather, the headwaters consit of several lakes and a large wetland. Although there is plenty of sediment in the river the bed and bank , the river typically can not mobilize these sediments because the resisting forces exceeds the impelling forces that would otherwise mobilze these sediments.   The river banks are tyically of one of two types as described below:

  • Tree lined with coarse sediments - the tree root system wrapping around coarse sediments provides for a strong embankment.
  • Well vegetated wetlands - The associated root systems produce erosion resistent banks and floodplains.
  Much of the river between The Oxbow and Beddington Lake is at a very low grade, on the order of 0.002 ft/ft, while the steeper sections of river are at roughly 0.005 ft/ft. In addition the log drive era left sections of river that are overwidened and occasionally armored. The armored beds are very erosion resistent, while the flat slopes and overwidened channels result in low impelling forces.   Where exceptions to the above discussion occur
