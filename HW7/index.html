P.1.1 to .1.5:   Water Discharge, etc.

Water Discharge (Q) via Inlet A (see Figure 1) appears to enter the SFT from
the bottom and is likely controlled by a valve or a variable speed pump.
Inlet B appears to be supplied by openings along the upstream wall of the flume

Slope (S) is likely controlled by tiliting the flume by a mechanical means.
The bed slope can also self adjust, for example, a sustained flood flow would
steepen the bed.

Sediment Discharge from the SFT was controlled by the outlet valve. To some extent,
the sediment discharge from the table can be controlled by how open/closed the valve is set.

Sediment Discharge (Qs) - Local: The local sediment transport rate varies in
reponse to the local flow rate and local bed slope.

Profile: The bed profile in the SFT is a function of past flow rates and the
erosion/deposition enviroments they produced.

Base Level: The base level elevation of the flume is controled by the outlet valve.
